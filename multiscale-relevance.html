Empirical data coming from complex systems exhibit a striking phenomenon – their underlying frequency distributions are broad, some obeying power laws (Fig 1).
Fig 1. Log-log plot of plots of rank versus frequency from a broad range of datasets.

Datasets that maximize relevance were found to have broad distributions (Cubero et al., 2019).
Indeed, when data obtained from spin models and restricted Boltzmann machines are optimally compressed, according to the minimum description length (Grünwald, 2007), I found that the typical samples, obtained from MCMC sampling of the dataset space, maximize relevance as well as exhibit broad distributions (Cubero et al., 2018).
To connect this with biological systems that ought to efficiently represent an external stimulus, I have shown that neurons that fire with maximal relevance at multiple timescales are neurons that can decode spatial and head directional information in freely-behaving rats (Cubero et al., 2020).
